Fall 2022 Conference

The conference was held at the unique Latchis Theater in Brattleboro Vermont.

The theme for the conference was: What our Site Research and Science Teach us about Northeast History.

View the program at Fall 2022 Program.

Presentations

We did record many of the presentations. Some of the videos will eventually be posted on our YouTube channel. Other videos will only be accessible after logging in on sitedb.org.

  • Land Acknowledgement and Shutesbury "Shrine" and Surrounds, Anne Marie Kittredge
  • The Long Hunter Wall: A Tennessee Snakenado, Dan Pezzoni
  • Chasing Vinland, the Search and Research of Norse in North America, Steve Voluckas
  • Recognizing the Diversity of Durable Ritual Offerings, Steve DiMarzo
  • Why Newport Tower is NOT an Old Windmill, Jim Egan
  • In Pursuit of Easy Site Reporting, Sydney Blackwell & Walter van Roggen
  • Evidence of the Construction and Use of Earthen Mounds and Enclosures in New England, Sarah Kohler
  • Celtic Monastic Oratories in New England? The Gungywamp-1 and Calendar II Chambers, Vance Tiede
  • Hidden Traces of Early Europeans in the Remains of a Drowned Forest, Terry Deveau
  • Stone Lines and Caribou Hunters: Identifying Ancient Hunting Structures Beneath the Great Lakes, Dr. John O'Shea
